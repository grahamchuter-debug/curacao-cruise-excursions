Why Cruise Passengers
Choose Curacao

Curacao is one of the Caribbean's most captivating cruise ports — a sun-drenched island where pastel Willemstad meets turquoise bays, and luxury shore days unfold at an unhurried Caribbean pace. From the remote white sands of Klein Curacao to sea turtle snorkeling and open-deck catamaran sailing, it is a destination that rewards travellers who want something genuinely special.

Whether you crave Klein Curacao day trips, guided turtle snorkeling, beach excursions at Cas Abao or Kenepa, a colourful Willemstad walking tour or a private luxury itinerary, Curacao delivers premium Caribbean moments without rushing your return to ship.

Most excursions are built around cruise schedules, so you can enjoy a full, relaxed day ashore and still be back at the pier with time to spare.

Cruise FAQ

Curacao Shore Excursion Questions

How much time do I need in Curacao port? ›

Most cruise ships are in port 7 to 10 hours. Klein Curacao day trips (full day) and Willemstad walking tours (2 to 3 hours) both work well if you confirm return times with your operator.

Can I visit Klein Curacao on a Curacao cruise day? ›

Yes — Klein Curacao is one of the most popular shore excursions from Curacao cruise port. Most tours depart early morning and return by mid-afternoon. Book early on busy port days.

Where can I swim with sea turtles in Curacao? ›

Guided turtle snorkeling tours visit sheltered bays where green and hawksbill turtles feed. Operators provide equipment and respect wildlife viewing guidelines. See our Curacao snorkeling tours guide for details.

What currency is used in Curacao? ›

The Netherlands Antillean guilder (ANG) is the official currency, but US dollars are widely accepted in Willemstad. Credit cards are accepted at most shops and restaurants near the cruise port.
