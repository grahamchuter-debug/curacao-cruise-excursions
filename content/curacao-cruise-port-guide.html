Port Orientation

Your First Look at Curacao

Curacao is one of the Caribbean's most distinctive cruise destinations — a Dutch Caribbean island where UNESCO-listed Willemstad, turquoise bays and luxury shore days define every port call. With a colourful capital, world-class snorkeling and a huge range of shore excursions, a day here can be as relaxed or as adventurous as you like.

This guide covers everything cruise passengers need, from the moment your ship arrives at Mega Pier or Mathey Wharf to when you're back on board. Ready to plan your perfect port day? Start with our Best Curacao Cruise Excursions guide.

Quick Answers for Cruise Passengers

🚢

Where will my ship dock?

Most large cruise ships dock at Mega Pier in Willemstad. Smaller vessels may use Mathey Wharf or anchor and tender passengers ashore near the waterfront.

⏰

How much time will I have?

Most itineraries give you 7 to 10 hours in port: usually 8am or 9am until 5pm or 6pm. Always confirm your ship's all aboard time and build in a 30-minute buffer.

💵

What currency is used?

The Netherlands Antillean guilder (ANG) is the official currency, but USD is widely accepted throughout Willemstad. Credit cards work at most shops and restaurants.

🚕

How do I get around?

Taxis and organised shore excursions are the simplest options. Most cruise passengers book excursions with included transport — ideal for Klein Curacao, beach and snorkeling tours.

📋

Do I need a passport?

A valid passport is required for most visitors to Curacao. Check your cruise line's documentation requirements before departure.

🤿

Is the snorkeling really that good?

Yes — Curacao offers sea turtle snorkeling, the famous Tugboat wreck and vibrant reef sites with exceptional visibility in crystal Caribbean water.

Port Terminals

Where Do Cruise Ships Dock?

Curacao's cruise terminals sit at the entrance to Willemstad's St. Anna Bay, placing you minutes from the colourful Handelskade waterfront and Queen Emma Bridge.

🚢

Mega Pier

Primary Terminal

The main cruise pier accommodates large ships at the Otrobanda side of St. Anna Bay. From the gangway, Willemstad's pastel Handelskade, excursion pickup points and waterfront cafés are a short walk or quick taxi ride away.

  • ✓Handles the Caribbean's largest cruise vessels
  • ✓Taxi stand and excursion departures at the pier
  • ✓Short transfer to Willemstad's UNESCO waterfront
  • ✓Boat operators for Klein Curacao depart from nearby marinas
🚤

Mathey Wharf & Tendering

Smaller Ships & Occasional

Smaller cruise ships may dock at Mathey Wharf in Punda, right in the heart of Willemstad. When the harbour is busy or for certain vessels, ships anchor offshore and ferry passengers ashore via tender boats — typically landing near the waterfront.

  • ✓Mathey Wharf puts you steps from Handelskade and the floating market
  • ✓Allow extra time for tender queues in the morning
  • ✓Book excursions through operators familiar with tender timing
  • ✓Return early — tender lines build before all aboard
